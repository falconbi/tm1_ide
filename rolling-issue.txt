Rolling 12/6/3 Consolidation — Off-by-One Issue
================================================

Current Approach
----------------
Uses day-based date serial filtering to determine which months are children
of the Rolling 12/6/3 consolidations.

  nCurStart = ParseDate(sCurStr, 'yyyyMMdd', nFmt) + 21916
  nSerial   = ParseDate(sMStr,    'yyyyMMdd', nFmt) + 21916

  Filter:  nSerial <= nCurStart AND nSerial > nCurStart - DAYS

  DAYS values:  365 (Rolling 12), 183 (Rolling 6), 90 (Rolling 3)

Where:
  - nCurStart = first day of the current period month (e.g. May 1, 2026)
  - nSerial   = first day of the candidate month

The Problem
-----------
Day-based boundaries don't align with month boundaries. Month lengths vary
(28-31 days), so a fixed number of days can include a different number of
months depending on where the cutoff falls.

Example: Current period = May 2026, 365 days back ≈ May 2025.
  - May 2025 is EXACTLY 365 days before May 2026
  - The filter "nSerial > nCurStart - 365" EXCLUDES May 2025
  - Result: June 2025 through May 2026 = 12 months (correct!)
  - BUT if months have varying lengths, the cutoff can shift by 1-2 days
  - A slightly different month configuration could include/exclude 1 extra

Similarly: 90 days can give 3 or 4 months depending on boundary alignment.

What Was Tried
--------------
Changed values:  367 → 365, 184 → 183, 93 → 90.
This reduced the overcount but doesn't solve the fundamental imprecision
of day-based windows versus month boundaries.

Potential Fix
-------------
Count months by fiscal position instead of days:

  nFisP = MOD(vCalM - pFYStartM + 12, 12) + 1   (fiscal position 1-12)

  For Rolling 12: include months whose fiscal position is within
  12 positions of the current period (wrapping across FY boundaries)

  For Rolling 6:  within 6 positions
  For Rolling 3:  within 3 positions

This approach counts months directly, avoiding the day-length variance issue.
